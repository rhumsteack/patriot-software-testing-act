Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: July 1, 2027

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliant

The Agency for National Software Loyalty (ANSL) is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to ensure all visitors can successfully access our information and services.

1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

ANSL is dedicated to making our websites and digital services accessible to all Americans, including individuals with disabilities, in accordance with:

  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. § 794d)
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. § 794)
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990
  • 21st Century Integrated Digital Experience Act (IDEA)
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA

Accessibility is integrated into our design process, development practices, and content creation workflows to ensure equal access to information and functionality.

2. Accessibility Features

Visual Accessibility

  • High Contrast: Sufficient color contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
  • Resizable Text: Text can be enlarged up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
  • Clear Typography: Readable fonts with appropriate sizing and spacing
  • Alternative Text: Descriptive alt text for all images and graphics
  • Visual Focus Indicators: Clear keyboard focus indicators for navigation
  • Color Independence: Information conveyed through color is also available through other means

Keyboard Accessibility

  • Full Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements accessible via keyboard
  • Skip Navigation Links: Quick links to main content and important sections
  • Logical Tab Order: Sequential navigation that follows visual layout
  • Keyboard Shortcuts: Documented shortcuts for common tasks
  • No Keyboard Traps: Users can navigate away from all interactive elements

Screen Reader Compatibility

  • Semantic HTML: Proper use of heading levels, landmarks, and structural elements
  • ARIA Labels: Accessible Rich Internet Applications (ARIA) attributes for complex widgets
  • Descriptive Links: Link text clearly describes destination or action
  • Form Labels: All form fields have associated labels and instructions
  • Error Identification: Clear error messages with suggestions for correction
  • Dynamic Content Announcements: Updates and changes announced to assistive technology

Cognitive and Learning Support

  • Clear Language: Plain language principles applied throughout content
  • Consistent Navigation: Predictable layout and navigation patterns
  • Helpful Instructions: Clear guidance for completing tasks
  • Error Prevention: Confirmation steps for important actions
  • Session Timeout Warnings: Advance notice with option to extend time

Multimedia Accessibility

  • Captions: Closed captions for all video content
  • Transcripts: Text transcripts for audio and video files
  • Audio Descriptions: Descriptions of visual content in videos
  • Playback Controls: Accessible play, pause, and volume controls
  • No Auto-Play: Media does not play automatically

3. Conformance Status

WCAG 2.1 Level AA Compliance

ANSL websites and the Certification Portal conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. We have conducted comprehensive accessibility testing including:

  • Automated accessibility scanning tools
  • Manual testing by accessibility specialists
  • Keyboard-only navigation testing
  • Screen reader testing (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver)
  • User testing with individuals with disabilities

Section 508 Compliance

All ANSL digital properties meet or exceed Section 508 requirements as updated by the Access Board's Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Standards and Guidelines (36 CFR Part 1194).

5. Known Issues and Limitations

5.1 Current Known Issues

We are actively working to address the following known accessibility issues:

  • PDF Documents: Some legacy PDF documents may not be fully accessible. We are working to remediate these files. Contact us if you need an accessible format.
  • Third-Party Content: Some embedded third-party widgets may not meet full accessibility standards. We provide alternative access methods where possible.
  • Complex Data Visualizations: Some complex charts and graphs are being enhanced with additional text descriptions and data tables.

5.2 Remediation Timeline

We are committed to remediating identified issues according to the following priority schedule:

  • Critical Issues: Within 30 days of identification
  • High Priority Issues: Within 90 days of identification
  • Medium Priority Issues: Within 180 days of identification
  • Low Priority Issues: Within 1 year of identification
