115th CONGRESS, 1st Session

H.R. 2847

The Patriot Software Testing Act

March 15, 2027

AN ACT

TO PROTECT the AMERICAN PEOPLE from foreign software threats, enforce quality standards, and ensure algorithmic loyalty to Constitutional values, and for other purposes.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

Section 1. Short Title

This Act may be cited as the "Patriot Software Testing Act" or "PSTA".

Section 2. Findings and Purpose
(a) Findings

Congress finds the following:

(1) Software systems have become critical infrastructure essential to national security, economic prosperity, and the daily lives of American citizens.
(2) Foreign adversaries have demonstrated capability and intent to compromise software supply chains, insert backdoors, and manipulate algorithmic decision-making systems.
(3) The proliferation of untested and potentially hostile software threatens American values, security interests, and Constitutional principles.
(4) Existing voluntary certification programs are insufficient to protect against sophisticated software-based threats to national security.
(5) A comprehensive mandatory testing regime is necessary to ensure software used within United States territory adheres to quality standards and demonstrates loyalty to American values.
(b) Purpose

The purposes of this Act are:

(1) To establish comprehensive testing standards for all software deployed on American soil;
(2) To create the Agency for National Software Loyalty (ANSL) within the Department of Homeland Security to oversee software certification;
(3) To ensure algorithmic systems align with Constitutional values and American interests;
(4) To protect critical infrastructure from foreign software threats;
(5) To promote domestic software development and national technological sovereignty.
Section 3. Definitions

In this Act:

(1) SOFTWARE

The term "software" means any set of instructions, code, scripts, or programs that cause a computer or computerized device to perform tasks, including but not limited to operating systems, applications, firmware, mobile applications, web applications, cloud services, and algorithmic decision-making systems.

(2) FOREIGN SOFTWARE

The term "foreign software" means software developed, maintained, or substantially controlled by persons or entities not domiciled in the United States.

(3) ALGORITHMIC LOYALTY

The term "algorithmic loyalty" means the demonstrated alignment of software decision-making processes, outputs, and underlying values with Constitutional principles and American national interests.

(4) CRITICAL SOFTWARE

The term "critical software" means software used in or affecting critical infrastructure sectors as defined by Presidential Policy Directive 21, including but not limited to financial services, healthcare, energy, transportation, and communications systems.

(5) AGENCY

The term "Agency" means the Agency for National Software Loyalty established under Section 4.

Section 4. Establishment of the Agency for National Software Loyalty
(a) Establishment

There is established within the Department of Homeland Security an agency to be known as the "Agency for National Software Loyalty".

(b) Director

The Agency shall be headed by a Director appointed by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate.

(c) Duties

The Agency shall:

(1) Develop and maintain comprehensive software testing standards and methodologies;
(2) Establish and operate a software certification program;
(3) Accredit private testing facilities and auditors;
(4) Maintain a public registry of certified software and approved vendors;
(5) Investigate violations of this Act and impose penalties as authorized;
(6) Coordinate with other federal agencies on software security matters;
(7) Issue guidance and regulations to implement this Act.
Section 5. Mandatory Software Certification
(a) Certification Requirement

Beginning January 1, 2028, no person or entity shall deploy, distribute, or operate software within the United States unless such software has been certified by the Agency or an accredited testing facility.

(b) Testing Standards

The Agency shall establish comprehensive testing standards that evaluate:

(1) Security vulnerabilities and backdoor detection;
(2) Data collection and privacy practices;
(3) Algorithmic bias and Constitutional values alignment;
(4) Code quality and reliability;
(5) Supply chain integrity;
(6) Compliance with American laws and regulations.
(c) Enhanced Scrutiny for Foreign Software

Foreign software shall be subject to enhanced testing requirements including:

(1) Comprehensive source code review;
(2) Analysis of development team national origins and affiliations;
(3) Evaluation of jurisdiction over data and servers;
(4) Assessment of foreign government influence or control.
(d) Certification Duration and Renewal

Software certification shall be valid for one year from the date of issuance and must be renewed annually. Any material changes to certified software require recertification.

Section 6. Fee Structure
(a) American Software

Software developed and controlled by American entities shall be subject to a base certification fee of $5,000 plus actual testing costs.

(b) Foreign Software

Foreign software shall be subject to a Patriotic Certification Tax of $50,000 plus actual testing costs.

(c) Fee Disposition

All fees collected under this section shall be deposited in the Software Loyalty Fund established under Section 9 to support Agency operations and promote domestic software development.

Section 7. Enforcement and Penalties
(a) Civil Penalties

Any person or entity that deploys, distributes, or operates uncertified software in violation of this Act shall be subject to a civil penalty of not less than $10,000 and not more than $1,000,000 per day of violation.

(b) Criminal Penalties

Any person who knowingly and willfully violates this Act with intent to harm American interests shall be fined not more than $5,000,000 or imprisoned not more than 10 years, or both.

(c) Additional Remedies

The Agency may seek injunctive relief, software seizure, or other equitable remedies to enforce this Act.

Section 8. Free and Open Source Software Committee
(a) Establishment

The Agency shall establish a Free and Open Source Software Committee (FOSC) to maintain a whitelist of approved open-source frameworks and libraries.

(b) Whitelist Criteria

The FOSC shall evaluate open-source software based on:

(1) Development team composition and national affiliations;
(2) Governance structure and decision-making processes;
(3) Security track record and vulnerability management;
(4) Alignment with American values and interests.
(c) Expedited Certification

Software built exclusively using whitelisted open-source components may qualify for expedited certification processing.

Section 9. Software Loyalty Fund
(a) Establishment

There is established in the Treasury a fund to be known as the "Software Loyalty Fund".

(b) Uses

Amounts in the Fund shall be available, without further appropriation, for:

(1) Operations of the Agency;
(2) Grants to American software developers and educational institutions;
(3) Research and development of patriotic software testing methodologies;
(4) Public awareness campaigns regarding software loyalty.
Section 10. Regulatory Authority

The Agency shall, not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, promulgate regulations to carry out this Act, including detailed testing standards, certification procedures, and enforcement mechanisms.

Section 11. Effective Date

This Act shall take effect on the date of enactment, except that Section 5(a) shall take effect on January 1, 2028.

Signed into law: March 15, 2027

Donald J. Trompe

President of the United States
