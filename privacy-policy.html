Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 1, 2027

Effective Date: July 1, 2027

Important Notice: The Agency for National Software Loyalty (ANSL) is committed to protecting the privacy and security of information collected through our official websites, certification portal, and services. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information in accordance with the Privacy Act of 1974, E-Government Act of 2002, and other applicable federal privacy laws.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

When you interact with ANSL services, we may collect:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, mailing address
  • Professional Information: Job title, company name, industry, professional affiliations
  • Account Credentials: Username, password (encrypted), security questions
  • Government IDs: Tax ID numbers, business registration numbers, security clearance information
  • Financial Information: Payment card details, bank account information for fee processing

1.2 Technical Information

We automatically collect technical data including:

  • IP addresses and device identifiers
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system information
  • Access times and referring website addresses
  • Pages viewed and links clicked
  • Cookies and tracking technologies (see Section 4)

1.3 Software Certification Data

For certification applications, we collect:

  • Complete software source code and binaries
  • Technical documentation and specifications
  • Development team information (names, nationalities, locations)
  • Ownership structure and control relationships
  • Foreign government relationships or contracts
  • Testing results and vulnerability assessments
  • Algorithmic Loyalty Scores and analysis

1.4 Communications

We retain records of:

  • Email correspondence with ANSL staff
  • Customer service inquiries and responses
  • Comments submitted through public forums
  • Newsletter subscriptions and preferences

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Primary Uses

ANSL uses collected information to:

  • Process Certifications: Review and evaluate software certification applications
  • Enforce Compliance: Monitor adherence to PSTA requirements and investigate violations
  • Maintain Registries: Operate public databases of certified software and approved vendors
  • Provide Services: Deliver requested information, support, and account access
  • Communicate: Send notifications about certification status, policy updates, and compliance deadlines
  • Improve Operations: Enhance website functionality, testing methodologies, and user experience

2.2 National Security Uses

⚠️ Important: Under authority of the PSTA and applicable national security laws, information collected by ANSL may be used for:
  • Threat assessment and counterintelligence activities
  • Foreign influence detection and analysis
  • Identification of software security vulnerabilities affecting critical infrastructure
  • Coordination with intelligence and law enforcement agencies

2.3 Research and Statistics

We may use aggregated, de-identified data for:

  • Statistical analysis of software certification trends
  • Public reporting on PSTA compliance rates
  • Research to improve testing methodologies
  • Policy development and regulatory guidance

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

3.1 Government Agencies

ANSL may share information with other federal agencies including:

  • Department of Homeland Security (parent agency)
  • Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
  • National Security Agency (NSA)
  • Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)
  • Department of Justice for enforcement actions
  • Intelligence Community agencies for national security purposes

3.2 Accredited Testing Facilities

Application data is shared with accredited testing facilities for certification evaluation purposes.

3.3 Public Disclosure

The following information is made publicly available:

  • Certified software names and versions
  • Company names of certified vendors
  • Certification tier and expiration dates
  • Enforcement actions and penalties
  • Watchlist of failed or revoked certifications

3.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by:

  • Court orders or subpoenas
  • Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests
  • Congressional oversight investigations
  • Inspector General audits
  • Law enforcement investigations

3.5 International Sharing

Under approved international agreements, limited information may be shared with:

  • Five Eyes intelligence alliance partners
  • NATO cybersecurity coordination bodies
  • Approved allied nation software security agencies

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

4.1 Cookie Usage

ANSL websites use cookies for:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Essential Cookies Enable core website functionality, user authentication, and security Session or 1 year
Analytics Cookies Measure website traffic and usage patterns using government-approved analytics 2 years
Preference Cookies Remember user settings and preferences 1 year
Security Cookies Detect suspicious activity and prevent fraud Session

4.2 Web Analytics

We use Digital Analytics Program (DAP), a government-wide analytics tool, to collect aggregate website usage statistics. DAP does not track individual users or create profiles.

4.3 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling essential cookies may prevent access to certain ANSL services, including the certification portal.

5. Data Security

5.1 Security Measures

ANSL implements comprehensive security controls including:

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 for data in transit, AES-256 for data at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access, multi-factor authentication, audit logging
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, DDoS protection
  • Physical Security: Controlled access facilities, video surveillance, security clearances
  • Security Assessments: Regular penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, compliance audits

5.2 Federal Compliance

ANSL systems comply with:

  • Federal Information Security Management Act (FISMA)
  • NIST SP 800-53 Security Controls (High Impact)
  • FedRAMP High authorization requirements
  • CJIS Security Policy where applicable

5.3 Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach, ANSL will:

  • Notify affected individuals within 72 hours of discovery
  • Report to US-CERT and appropriate oversight bodies
  • Provide credit monitoring services if Social Security numbers are compromised
  • Conduct thorough investigation and implement corrective actions

6. Data Retention

6.1 Retention Periods

Data Type Retention Period Authority
Certification Applications 7 years after final action NARA Schedule N1-563-10-01
Source Code Submissions 5 years after certification expiration PSTA § 8(c)
Enforcement Records 10 years after case closure 28 CFR § 16.30
Website Analytics 2 years OMB M-17-06

6.2 Secure Disposal

Data is securely destroyed in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 media sanitization guidelines.

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Access and Correction

Under the Privacy Act, you have the right to:

  • Request access to records about you maintained by ANSL
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Receive an accounting of certain disclosures

7.2 FOIA Requests

You may submit Freedom of Information Act requests for ANSL records. Note that certain information may be exempt from disclosure under FOIA exemptions (b)(1) through (b)(9), particularly records related to national security or ongoing enforcement actions.

7.3 Opt-Out Options

You may opt out of:

  • Marketing communications (does not apply to compliance notifications)
  • Non-essential cookies through browser settings
  • Newsletter subscriptions

7.4 Limitations

Note: Certain rights may be limited when information is maintained for law enforcement, national security, or regulatory compliance purposes. ANSL will review each request on a case-by-case basis.

8. Children's Privacy

ANSL websites and services are not directed to children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information to ANSL, please contact our Privacy Officer immediately.

9. Third-Party Links

ANSL websites may contain links to third-party websites for reference purposes. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of external sites. When leaving ansl.gov, you are subject to the privacy policies of the destination site.

10. Privacy Act System of Records

ANSL maintains the following Privacy Act Systems of Records:

  • DHS/ANSL-001: Software Certification and Compliance Records
  • DHS/ANSL-002: Enforcement and Investigation Files
  • DHS/ANSL-003: Accredited Testing Facility Records
  • DHS/ANSL-004: Public Portal User Accounts

System of Records Notices (SORNs) are published in the Federal Register and available at www.federalregister.gov.

11. Updates to This Policy

ANSL may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or operational needs. Material changes will be posted prominently on our website at least 30 days before taking effect. Continued use of ANSL services after changes indicates acceptance of the updated policy.
